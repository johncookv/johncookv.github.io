My Story

After working for over 13 years in education, I felt the desire to go back to school, not only to obtain my Bachelors degree, but to do it in a field I was passionate about: Software Engineering. Studying full-time while being a full-time educator, husband, and father of three children was overwhelming at times, but I eventually graduated with my Bachelors degree from Brigham Young University - Idaho in July 2017. Now I get to enjoy a rewarding and fulfilling career.

But there is one thing about the tech industry that is a bit unsettling. During my time in my undergraduate program and within the industry, I have yet to meet another Pacific Islander. I researched demographics from the top tech companies and noticed a depressing trend. The number of Pacific Islander employees would usually make up less than a tenth of a percent of the company, with the number of employees in actual tech-related (non-clerical) careers being either zero or in the single digits. I decided to take on the goal of increasing the number of Pacific Islanders in the tech industry. I plan on joining outreach programs and reaching out to tech companies to share my findings. Stay tuned on updates on these efforts.
